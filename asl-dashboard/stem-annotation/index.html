Construct Definitions

Lexical Sign — an established, conventional sign

Fingerspelling — a combination of manual alphabet letters to represent a written/spoken word, acronym, name, or abbreviation

Loansign — a borrowed form, typically from fingerspelling/written language contact, that has become entrenched enough to function as a lexical item.

Name Sign — a sign used as a person, place, or entity name to refer to a particular individual, place, or entity.

Number Sign — a sign whose primary function is to express a numerical value or quantify a noun.

Compound — a single lexical item formed from two lexical items that function together as one word.

Gesture — a non-lexical, imagistic communicative movement that is not treated as an established lexical sign.

Neologism — a newly created sign for a new or newly salient concept, often not yet fully stabilized or standardized across signers.

Classifier — a productive sign/construction in which handshape and movement/location schematically represent a referent class, its shape, handling, spatial relation, or enactment, rather than naming it with a fixed lexical sign.

Classifier Subtypes

Body — a classifier in which the signer’s body represents and enacts the referent’s action. Example: “puts arm around friend”

Body Part — a classifier in which a specific body part of the signer represents the corresponding body part of the referent doing the action. Example: “taps foot”

Descriptive — a classifier used to describe the visible properties of a referent, especially size, shape, or appearance. Example: “curly hair” (aka Size and Shape Specifiers)

Instrument — a classifier showing how an object is manipulated or used, usually by the hands. Example: “turning a steering wheel”

Locative — a classifier showing where an object is located, and often also how it moves in space. Example: “leaf drifting to the ground”

Plural — a classifier marking multiple referents, whether the number is exact or non-exact. Example: “long line of people”

Semantic — a classifier whose handshape stands for a semantic class of referents, such as person, animal, or vehicle. Example: “person walking quickly”
