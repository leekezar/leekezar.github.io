about me

I am a third-year Ph.D. student at the University of Southern California and a Graduate Research Assistant at Information Sciences Institute. My work improves neural language modeling with psychological and linguistic theory, with emphasis on understanding gestures, facial expressions, knowledge, and intent.

  • sign language linguistics
  • affective computing

selected projects

Sign Language Understanding

I use state-of-the-art pose estimation and facial expression recognition to produce linguistically-informed representations of American Sign Language phonemes. I then use semi- and self-supervised learning techniques to approximate the meaning of phoneme combinations.
